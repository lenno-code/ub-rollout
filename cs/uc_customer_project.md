## Case Study: Introduction of X-as-Code with Sphinx-Needs (useblocks open-source tool) in an Automotive Context (Estimated time: 2–4 hours)

### **Objective**

You take on the role of a rollout engineer supporting an automotive customer in transitioning from their current Excel-based specification and development documentation to a modern **X-as-Code approach using Sphinx-Needs**.

The goal is to clearly demonstrate to the customer through a demo:

a) the benefits,

b) the concepts, and

c) the possibilities of this way of working.

### **Initial Situation**

An automotive customer currently manages their software development requirements in an Excel file.

The customer wants to:

- move away from Excel
- transition to a version-controlled, scalable, and automated system
- explore an X-as-Code approach using **Sphinx + Sphinx-Needs**
- understand the potential of AI in this context

You are provided with:

- an Excel list
- the Sphinx-Needs online documentation

### **Your Task**

### **1. Concept & Analysis**

- Analyze the customer’s current situation
- Identify which **Sphinx-Needs features and concepts** are most relevant for this customer

**Outcome:**

→ Justify your selection with a structured explanation

### **2. Technical Implementation (Demo)**

Create a **Sphinx-Needs project** that:

- meaningfully represents the content of the Excel file
- demonstrates the key advantages of X-as-Code

**Outcome:**

→ Showcase the most important features based on your analysis in step 1

→ Provide a clear and understandable presentation within the IDE

### **3. Presentation Concept**

Adress the needs and interest of the customer in a structured way.

**Outcome:**

→ Create a simple outline (bullet points) describing how you would present your demo to the customer
